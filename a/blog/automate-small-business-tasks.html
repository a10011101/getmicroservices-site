10 Small Business Tasks You Should Automate Right Now

The average SME owner spends 23 hours a week on repetitive, manual tasks. Most of them could be automated in under a day. Here are the 10 highest-ROI places to start.

1. Invoice Chasing

Time saved: 2–4 hours/week. Overdue invoice detected → reminder at day 3 → firmer email at day 7 → Slack alert at day 14. We recovered £8,000 for one client automating exactly this.

2. Lead Follow-Up

Time saved: 1–3 hours/week. Form submission → CRM updated → personalised email within 5 minutes → task for sales team. First 5-minute response rate is 21× higher than waiting an hour.

3. Booking Confirmations

Time saved: 1–2 hours/week. Booking confirmed → confirmation email + calendar invite → reminder 24h before → no-show follow-up. Fully hands-off.

4. Weekly Report Generation

Time saved: 2–5 hours/week. Pull from Analytics, Shopify, ad accounts → format → email Monday morning. No manual copying in 2026.

5. Social Media Scheduling

Time saved: 1–2 hours/week. Draft in Sheets/Notion → automation schedules via Buffer or API. Batch-create weekly, everything else runs itself.

6. Customer Onboarding

Time saved: 3–6 hours/week. New client → welcome email → accounts created → checklist in Asana → intro call booked. 45 minutes per client → 0 minutes.

7. Low Stock Alerts

Prevents costly stockouts. Product hits threshold → Slack alert → draft PO created. 30 minutes to build.

8. Expense Tracking

Time saved: 1–2 hours/week. Forward receipt → AI parses it → expense logged in accounting software. No more end-of-month archaeology.

9. Meeting Notes + Actions

Time saved: 30–60 min/day. Recording → transcription → GPT-4o extracts actions → added to PM tool + emailed to participants.

10. Support Triage

Time saved: 2–4 hours/day. Incoming message → AI classifies → routes to right person with summary → auto-responds to FAQs. This is the AI automation we build most often.
